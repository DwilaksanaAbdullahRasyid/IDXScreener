## Why These Changes Work

### Problem (V3.4):
- Strategy correctly identifies 50% of reversals (good entry accuracy)
- But average win (0.865R) << average loss (1.0R)
- Negative expectancy: -0.048R per trade → loses money long-term
- TP3 hit rate too low (7%) → trades not trending long enough

### Solution (V3.5):
- **Option 1 fixes the math:** Wider targets mean higher average win (1.30R > 1.0R)
- **Option 2 helps execution:** Longer holds allow price to reach wider targets
- **Combined effect:** Profitable strategy (+0.15-0.18R expectancy)

## Technical Details

### TP Hit Rate Mechanics (V3.4 vs V3.5)

**V3.4 Example Trade:**
- Entry: 5000 IDR
- SL: 4925 IDR (risk 75 IDR = 1R)
- TP1: 5075 IDR (1R from entry) → hit 51% of time
- TP2: 5150 IDR (2R from entry) → hit 22% of time
- TP3: 5225 IDR (3R from entry) → hit 7% of time

**V3.5 Example Trade (Same Entry/SL):**
- Entry: 5000 IDR
- SL: 4925 IDR (risk 75 IDR = 1R, unchanged)
- TP1: 5112 IDR (1.5R from entry) → hit ~40% of time (slightly lower)
- TP2: 5225 IDR (3R from entry) → hit ~20% of time (similar)
- TP3: 5337 IDR (4.5R from entry) → hit ~7% of time (same)

**Result:** Despite slightly lower TP hit rates, profits per hit are 50% higher → positive expected value.
